# GESTURIA

## A world where sign language is a first-class citizen of the internet.

---

## 1. Why Gesturia exists

In Cameroon there are more than **300,000 deaf people**. Fewer than 1% of them have full access to formal education. The classrooms exist. The teachers exist. The technology exists. What is missing is the **bridge** — the moment where a hearing world stops being a wall and starts being a place a deaf child can walk into.

Every day, deaf children sit in classrooms watching lips they cannot read, listening to lessons they cannot hear, in a language nobody around them signs back. Every day, deaf adults scroll social media, watch videos, and read news that was never made for them. Every day, hearing parents love deaf children they cannot fully speak with.

Gesturia is not a translation app. Gesturia is the answer to that quiet, daily exclusion.

We do not build tools that "help the disabled." We build tools that make the disability go away — because the disability was never in the person. The disability was in the platform, the classroom, the browser, the video call. It was in every piece of software that assumed everyone could hear.

Gesturia rebuilds the software.

---

## 2. The name

**Gesturia** — from _gesture_ (the language) and _-uria_ (a place, a territory). A country of gestures. A homeland for signed language, on the internet.

It is not a diminutive. It is not a "sign language app." It is a place. When a deaf student in Yaoundé opens a Gesturia product, they are not being served. They are home.

That framing changes everything about how we build. Nothing in Gesturia is a plugin bolted onto a hearing person's app. Every surface — the LMS, the learner app, the translator, the certificate, the browser extension — is designed from the deaf user outward, with the hearing world adapting to _them_.

We inverted the default. That is the entire product philosophy in one sentence.

---

## 3. The avatars — Gest and Uria

**Gest** and **Uria** are our two signing avatars. They are not mascots. They are not decorative. They are the face and hands of the entire brand.

- **Gest** — the everyman. Calm, warm, slightly older. He is the teacher, the translator, the patient explainer. When a hearing parent wants to sign "good morning" to their deaf son for the first time, Gest shows them how, and does not judge them for taking six tries to get the handshape right.
- **Uria** — the peer. Younger, curious, a little playful. She is the friend, the study partner, the one who signs a joke back to a student when they answer a question correctly. She is the face of Gestsolo. When she gets a comprehension question right, students want to keep going.

Gest and Uria are voiced by our translation pipeline — the same 4-model AI stack that powers every Gesturia product. Their signing is not pre-recorded video. Every sign they produce is generated in real time from text, using the same infrastructure that will one day run at national scale.

They are the **only interface** to sign language in our products. When a user opens any Gesturia surface, they see Gest or Uria. When they close it, they close a real relationship. This is deliberate. Deaf children who grow up with Gest and Uria will remember them the way a hearing child remembers Sesame Street. That is what a brand is for.

## 4. The product family

Gesturia is one platform, one AI, one brand — served through five products. Each product exists because there is a specific human moment where the hearing world fails a deaf person, and each product is the answer to that specific moment.

We do not build products because they are technically interesting. We build products because a specific person, in a specific country, on a specific day, cannot do something they should be able to do. Each product below has that person in mind.

---

### 4.1 Gestlingua — the translator

**"Say anything. See it signed."**

The public face of Gesturia. A translator. You type or speak, in English or French — Gest or Uria signs it back to you, in real 3D, on your screen.

Not video clips. Not pre-recorded loops. Every sentence is generated fresh by our 4-model AI pipeline: text → gloss → 3D pose → smoothed motion → facial expression. The avatar's eyebrows raise on a question. Their head shakes on a negation. Their hands move at the actual speed a deaf signer would move them.

Gestlingua is who we are to the world. When a journalist writes about Gesturia, this is what they screenshot. When a minister wants to see what we do, this is what we open first. When a hearing parent wants to sign "I love you" to their child for the first time, this is where they come.

We are not competing with Google Translate. Google Translate does not sign. Nobody signs at scale on the internet. Gestlingua is the first product in the world that makes sign language a first-class output of a translation system — not a subtitle, not a captioning workaround, but a native, generated, real-time performance by a person on your screen.

The vision: **every sign language in the world, generated on demand, by an avatar the community trusts.** We start with American Sign Language and Langue des Signes Africaine Francophone. We do not stop there.

---

### 4.2 Gestaula — the school

**"A classroom where every child is fluent."**

Gestaula is the LMS — the learning management system — for schools that serve deaf students. It is not "an LMS with accessibility features." It is an LMS where the medium of instruction _is_ sign language.

Teachers open Gestaula and assign a lesson. The lesson auto-signs. Students open the lesson and Gest or Uria walks them through it in their own language. When the student answers a question, their webcam sends landmarks to our backend, our AI grades the sign across all five ASL parameters (handshape, palm orientation, location, movement, non-manual markers), and gives them structured feedback in seconds. The teacher's dashboard shows exactly which students are struggling with which handshapes, which lessons the class as a whole hasn't grasped, which classrooms are outperforming which.

The teacher does not need to know sign language to teach a signed class. That is the revolution. A hearing teacher, using Gestaula, can run a fully-signed lesson because Gest and Uria are doing the signing. The teacher's job becomes what teaching should be — designing curriculum, spotting confusion, mentoring individual students — while the AI handles the language layer.

**INJS Yaoundé — Institut National des Jeunes Sourds — is our first school.** MINESEC — the Cameroonian Ministry of Secondary Education — is our first partner. Every course in Gestaula is aligned to the national curriculum. Every certificate that comes out of Gestaula is designed to be recognized by MINESEC as proof of proficiency. This is not a startup pilot. This is the beginning of a **national deaf education system**, delivered digitally.

When the ministry announces free deaf education as a right — and they will — Gestaula is the software that fulfills that promise at scale.

---

### 4.3 Gestsolo — the learner

**"Duolingo for hands."**

Gestsolo is for the deaf person who wants to learn a new sign language, the hearing parent of a deaf child who wants to learn their child's language, and the hearing student who is curious and wants to add sign language to their skills.

It is playful, gamified, mobile-first. Streaks. Hearts. XP. Levels. Leaderboards. Speed challenges. Story mode with signed narratives. Achievement unlocks with confetti. Uria walks you through your first lesson. She does not lecture. She jokes. She waits when you struggle. She celebrates when you get it. This is not a course. This is a friendship.

The gamification is not incidental. Deaf students are used to educational products that are drab, clinical, "special." Gestsolo refuses that. If a hearing kid gets a bright, playful, addictive app for learning Spanish, a deaf kid deserves the same energy for learning ASL. If a hearing adult can casually pick up French on their commute, a hearing parent should be able to casually pick up the language their deaf daughter uses every day — on the same commute, on the same phone.

We built streaks that count. Achievements that unlock live. A parent mode that lets Mme Béatrice see her daughter Léa's progress in the same dashboard as her own. A speed challenge that lets a Form 5 student race the clock signing every animal name she can remember. A story mode that walks a beginner through _La Tortue et le Léopard_ in Cameroonian LSAF, with comprehension questions at the end.

Every one of these features is in production. Every one is tied to the same XP + streak + achievement plumbing so that when a student finishes a story, their streak advances, their XP updates, their next achievement fires — all in one atomic response. This is the difference between a demo and a product.

Gestsolo is how we reach millions of learners. Gestaula is how we reach every school. **Together they are how sign language stops being a niche language and becomes a mainstream one.**

---

### 4.4 Gestify — the certificate

**"Proof that speaks for itself."**

Gestify is our certification layer. When a Gestsolo learner completes a course, or a Gestaula student passes an end-of-year assessment, a **Gestificate** is auto-issued. GST-YYYYMM-XXXXXXXX. Uniquely numbered. Verifiable at gesturia.cm/verify/<number> by anyone in the world, with no login, forever.

The public verify endpoint is not a marketing gimmick. It is the point. When an employer in Douala receives a CV that says "certified in ASL by Gesturia," they can scan a QR code and confirm it in three seconds. When a Canadian university admits a deaf Cameroonian student who claims Level 3 LSAF proficiency, they can verify the exact score, the exact date, and the exact issuing institution. When MINESEC audits our platform, every certificate we've ever issued is on the public record.

This is how you build **legitimacy** for a language that has spent centuries being dismissed as informal. When a certificate says "Gestificate: American Sign Language" and it can be verified in three seconds by anyone on earth, sign language stops being a hobby and starts being a **credential**.

We chose the name Gestify — from _gesture_ + _certify_ — deliberately. It should sound like Spotify, like Shopify, like something familiar and international. Because that is what we want signed-language proficiency to become: as ordinary a credential to hold as a driver's license, and as easy to prove.

### 4.5 Gest-X — the extension

**"The internet, signed. Everywhere. Automatically."**

This is the product that changes the game.

Every other Gesturia product is a destination — you go to Gestlingua, you go to Gestaula, you go to Gestsolo. **Gest-X is the opposite.** Gest-X is a browser extension. You install it once. From that moment on, **every website in the world is signable.**

You are scrolling TikTok. A creator posts a video, no captions, spoken French. Gest-X detects the audio, transcribes it in real time, sends the text to our backend, and Uria signs it in a small floating window in the corner of your screen. Synced to the video's timing. You never miss a beat.

You are reading a BBC article. You right-click the paragraph. "Sign this." Gest signs the paragraph aloud, in ASL or LSAF, with the article's text scrolling beside him. You catch every nuance because the eyebrow-raise on the question mark is right, the head-shake on the negation is right, the pace is right.

You are on a Zoom call with a hearing colleague. Gest-X captions their speech and signs it in a picture-in-picture window. You reply by signing back into your webcam — Gest-X reads your signs, transcribes them into text, and the hearing colleague sees the caption on their screen. **A hearing-deaf conversation, real-time, with no interpreter, on a stock Zoom call.**

You are on WhatsApp Web. A friend sends you a voice note. Gest-X plays it as signed video, silently, in a sidebar. You reply by signing to your webcam. Gest-X converts it to text and pastes it into the chat.

**The web was built for hearing people.** Every social platform, every video site, every news outlet, every messaging app assumed audio and text as first-class formats and treated sign language as an afterthought — if it treated it at all. Gest-X inverts that. It layers sign language on top of the existing web, without the existing web needing to change.

We are not asking TikTok to add sign language. We are not asking BBC to hire an interpreter. We are not asking Zoom to build accessibility. **We are making all of them signable, from the outside, in one install.**

The vision is enormous:

- **Every video on the internet, signed on demand.**
- **Every article, signed on demand.**
- **Every voice message, signed on demand.**
- **Every livestream, signed in real time.**
- **Every hearing-to-deaf conversation, mediated by Gest-X, invisible to both sides.**

This is not accessibility as a compliance checkbox. This is accessibility as an **operating system layer**. The internet becomes a fully bilingual place — spoken/written and signed, on every page, for every deaf user, forever, with no permission from the original site required.

Gest-X is what makes Gesturia not a company but an **infrastructure**. Gestlingua is the destination people visit to see what we do. Gest-X is the invisible layer that follows them everywhere else.

## 5. One AI, five products

Every product above is powered by the same backend. The same 4-model pipeline: text-to-gloss (T5-base), gloss-to-pose (Transformer + ST-GCN), motion smoother (BiLSTM), facial expression generator (cross-attention). The same 288-dimension pose format. The same avatar meshes for Gest and Uria. The same PostgreSQL schema of users, courses, lessons, exercises, certificates, streaks.

**We built one AI. We ship it in five ways.**

That is the strategic bet. Every competitor in the accessibility space builds a single point solution — a captioning tool, a specific-purpose avatar, a narrow classroom app. We built the horizontal platform first. Now every product on top of it — Gestlingua, Gestaula, Gestsolo, Gestify, Gest-X — shares the same brain, so improving one improves all five.

When our AI's palm orientation scoring gets 5% more accurate next quarter, Gestsolo gets 5% better feedback, Gestaula's teacher dashboards get 5% cleaner data, Gest-X's real-time signing on WhatsApp looks 5% more natural. All at once. Zero extra engineering.

**This is how you build a moat in AI.** Not a bigger model. A better distribution. Every deaf user in Cameroon becomes a data point that improves every product we ship, forever.

## 6. What Gesturia will NOT do

We are as defined by what we refuse to build as by what we ship.

**We will not build gimmicks.** No "AI translator" that produces jerky, unnatural signing. Every avatar performance is either at native-signer quality or it is not shipped. Our reputation is our brand.

**We will not build for hearing people first.** Every design decision starts with a deaf user's experience. Hearing accessibility comes second, as a byproduct, not as a target.

**We will not centralize on one sign language.** ASL is where we start because it has the most training data. LSAF is where our mission begins because it is the language of the country we serve. We are building the infrastructure so any sign language in the world can be added — DGS (German), BSL (British), FSL (Filipino), Auslan (Australian) — as data becomes available.

**We will not sell out the community.** Deaf users' data is not for sale. Their signs are not training material for third-party companies. Their certificates are not monetized behind paywalls. If Gestsolo has ads, they will be for our own products. If Gestaula has premium tiers, the free tier will still teach a child to sign.

**We will not lose the human center.** Every product ships with Gest and Uria — not a logo, not a chatbot, not an interface. Faces. Hands. People who remember your streak and celebrate when you unlock an achievement. This is what makes Gesturia feel like home instead of like software.

## 7. The trajectory

**Today** — Backend and AI are production-grade at code level. Training run is the immediate next gate. Demo storyboard walks end-to-end on canned data for the MINESEC pitch. INJS Yaoundé is our first school. Cameroon is our first country.

**In 6 months** — Trained models live on HuggingFace Hub. Gestlingua public at gesturia.cm. Gestaula deployed to five schools in Yaoundé and Douala. Gestsolo on iOS and Android. First 1000 Gestificates issued. Gest-X in closed beta on Chrome.

**In 18 months** — Gest-X shipped to Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. LSAF fluent. Deployed in every INJS-affiliated school in Cameroon. MINESEC-endorsed curriculum in every classroom. First hearing-to-deaf Zoom calls mediated end-to-end by Gest-X, invisibly, at scale.

**In 5 years** — Every major sign language in Africa modeled and served. Every deaf child in Central and West Africa has a device with Gesturia on it. Every hearing parent of a deaf child in the region has learned to sign through Gestsolo. Every browser ships with something like Gest-X pre-installed or trivially available. Sign language is a first-class citizen of the internet.

**In 10 years** — The default assumption on any new web platform is that it will be signable. Not because the platform builds sign support in — because Gest-X (or something built on our AI) has already made it signable. Deaf isolation on the internet is over. A generation of Cameroonian deaf children have grown up bilingual, credentialed, employed, and connected to a hearing world that finally, at last, can hear them back.

That is the vision. It is not modest. **A modest vision would be an insult to the users we are building for.**
