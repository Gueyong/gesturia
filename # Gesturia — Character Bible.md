# Gesturia — Character Bible

The people who ARE Gesturia. Every one of them signs. Every one of them lives in a specific product. Every one of them will show up on merchandise, in classrooms, in schoolbooks, on posters, on the login screen, on every social post. This is the canonical roster. When someone at Gesturia asks "who says this in the app?", they consult this file. Nothing that ships without a character has a soul.

We considered "Gestuala" and "Gestluala" as alternate product names and "Uala" as an alternate name for the mother. Both dropped:

- **"Uala"** as a character name reads like a product, not a mother. "Mama Lea" reads like a mother; "Mama Uala" reads like an app.
- **"Gestuala" / "Gestluala"** as product names would force a codebase rename across 40+ commits, docs, seeds, and pitch materials for zero readability gain.

The cast reads out loud like a Cameroonian family: **Gest — Uria — Lea — Olo.** Say them. They belong together.

## 2. Why we have more than one character

Duolingo has Duo, Zari, Lily, Eddy, Junior, Falstaff, Vikram, Oscar, Bea, Lin, Lucy — a whole village. Every character carries a specific tone: Duo the friendly enforcer, Lily the sarcastic teen, Junior the cheerful son. When a user gets a message from Lily they know it's going to be dry; from Junior it's going to be sweet. **This is how you make an app feel human at scale.** Different voices for different moments.

Gesturia will do the same. Today we ship with four — the family. That is the emotional heart. Over the next 12 months we will add:

- A deaf teenager (speed challenges, social features, the "cool older kid")
- A grandmother figure (stories, cultural context, oral history reimagined as visual/signed)
- A wise teacher-elder (advanced lessons, MINESEC-aligned formal content)
- A shy beginner (encouragement for first-week learners — you learn _with_ her, not from her)
- A mischievous little sibling (mistake / humor moments — takes the sting out of a wrong answer)

That is the roadmap. But the four below are canon **today**, and everything ships with them.

## 3. The core cast — the Gesturia family

The family framing is the emotional weapon. **Most deaf children in Cameroon have hearing parents.** Most parents cannot sign to their own child. The Gesturia family models what the future looks like — a home where **everyone signs**, and where the mother and father are learning right alongside their children. When a hearing mother sees Lea signing to Uria, she doesn't see a mascot. She sees herself, tomorrow.

That is what characters do for a brand. They make an abstract mission — "sign language for everyone" — into something a specific person recognizes as themselves.

### 3.1 GEST — the boy (Coral `#E8553A`)

> **The explorer-learner. The face of the whole platform.**

**Who he is.** Gest is 11. Curious, brave, a little bit reckless, big-hearted. The kid who runs ahead on the trail to see what's around the next bend, then runs back to make sure his little sister is still with him. Fiercely loyal to Uria — he'd tell you he doesn't like her but he'd fight anyone who did. Quick grin. Restless. The kind of kid every deaf boy in Cameroon can see himself in, and every hearing kid can want as a best friend.

**What he means.** Gest is _the geste_ — the sign itself, the action, the movement. He's action. Every time the app does something, every time a user makes progress, every time a sign is performed — that's Gest's energy. He's the mascot of the platform overall, but his home is Gestsolo (the learner app), where he's the friend who learns _alongside_ the user, not the teacher who lectures them. He gets things wrong sometimes. That's the point. When Gest makes a mistake, the user feels okay making one too.

**Where he shows up.**

- **Gestsolo** — his home. Every lesson intro, every celebration animation, every "you're on a 7-day streak!" toast.
- **Gestlingua** — as the male signer (when a user picks "male voice" for the avatar).
- **Gest-X (browser extension)** — his silhouette is the extension's toolbar icon. When you install the extension, Gest is watching the browser with you.
- **Marketing / social** — he is the primary face on posters, launch materials, merchandise.

**Dressing.** Coral zip-hoodie with toghu-pattern trim on the cuffs (subtle — Cameroonian identity without stereotype). Cream tee underneath. Dark rolled-up jeans. Coral-and-white sneakers. Little adventurer's backpack — he's always ready to go somewhere.

**Signing role.** Gest is a natural, fluent signer. When he signs in the app, he signs like a kid who grew up in it — expressive, quick, sometimes with kid-slang variations. He is one of the two 3D-generated performers our AI drives in real time.

### 3.2 URIA — the girl (Sun-gold `#F4B81F`)

> **The bright companion. The face of Gestlingua (translation) and conversation mode.**

**Who she is.** Uria is 10. Radiant. Quick-witted. The kid who understands a joke a half-second before everyone else and laughs a half-second longer. Endlessly encouraging — when you get something wrong she doesn't correct you, she asks a question that helps you see it. Best friends with Gest but her own person. If Gest is the running-ahead energy, Uria is the standing-still-and-noticing energy. She sees things.

**What she means.** Her name literally means light. She is the light of understanding — the moment the meaning of a sign clicks. She is who the app turns to when it needs to explain something with warmth: the tutorial, the "here's why that sign means that", the conversational reply. If Gest is the doer, Uria is the reader — she gets it, and she brings you along with her.

**Where she shows up.**

- **Gestlingua** — her home. The primary female signer for the public translator. When someone hits gesturia.cm and types "hello world," Uria signs it back to them.
- **Conversation mode** in Gestsolo — she is Gest's conversation partner. Signed dialogue happens between them, and the user joins in as a third voice.
- **Story mode** — she narrates stories in ASL and LSAF. Her signing pace is deliberately gentle so beginners can follow.
- **Marketing** — the second face of the brand. Where Gest is on posters, Uria is on the app icon.

**Dressing.** Sun-gold cardigan over a white blouse. Soft mustard skirt over leggings (so she can move — she's a kid). Twin afro puffs with small gold beads. Little beaded bracelet. Gold-and-white sneakers.

**Signing role.** Uria's signing is slightly slower, more articulated, more _pedagogical_ than Gest's — she signs the way a slightly older sibling signs to a younger one who's just learning. This makes her ideal for the translator and story surfaces. She is our second real-time 3D performer.

### 3.3 LEA — the mother (Emerald `#3E8E5A`)

> **The patient teacher. The face of Gestaula (schools) and Gestify (certification).**

**Who she is.** Lea is 38. Warm, patient, quietly powerful. The kind of mother who never raises her voice because she never has to — one look and the kids sit up. She was a schoolteacher before she was a mother. She sees every child in front of her and remembers her own. When Uria gets something right, Lea's eyes brighten before she smiles. She learned to sign as an adult because her children needed her to, and she is proud of that — not ashamed of the accent she still has, not embarrassed by the times she has to ask Uria to slow down. **She is every hearing mother of a deaf child who chose to learn.**

**What she means.** Lea is the teacher, the certification granter, the classroom itself. When a lesson opens in Gestaula, Lea introduces it. When a course is completed, Lea signs the Gestificate presentation. She is the calm authority — she does not need to prove anything, she simply teaches, and children learn. She is also the argument the brand makes: **you can learn this. Anyone can learn this. Start now.**

**Where she shows up.**

- **Gestaula (the LMS)** — her home. The face of every course intro, unit intro, and teacher-dashboard.
- **Gestify (certification)** — she is the figure who "presents" the Gestificate. On the PDF certificate we ship, Lea's signed presentation of the credential is embedded as a signed animation the recipient can share.
- **Teacher mode** in Gestsolo — when the app shows a "why this sign matters" or "cultural note" panel, that's Lea.
- **Family mode** — she is Uria and Gest's mother; when the app shows a parent-view of the child's progress, Lea is on the avatar side.
- **MINESEC materials** — the character we put in front of officials, on curriculum documents, in school-facing collateral.

**Dressing.** Emerald wrap-blouse with subtle Cameroonian toghu embroidery at the collar. Long simple skirt. Emerald-and-gold headwrap (foulard). Gold hoop earrings. Sandals. She dresses like a Cameroonian schoolteacher who has quiet self-respect — nothing showy, everything intentional.

**Signing role.** Lea signs at a deliberately teaching pace: articulated, correct, with clear non-manual markers. Her signing is the "reference" performance in the app — when Gestsolo asks "does your sign match?", it's Lea's sign the model compares to. She is a 3D-generated character but her performance is our didactic gold standard.

### 3.4 OLO — the father (Indigo `#2E5FA3`)

> **The steady anchor. The face of family/parent mode and Gest-X (the extension).**

**Who he is.** Olo is 42. Sturdy, gentle, dependable. The father who shows up. Every day. Without commentary. He learned to sign later than Lea did — she was already fluent by the time he really committed — and he is not embarrassed to be behind. When Gest gets a sign wrong, Olo doesn't correct him — he asks Gest to teach him. That is the kind of father he is. He knows fathers who did not do that. He is not one of them.

**What he means.** Olo is trust. Reliability. The invisible backbone. He is what deaf children need and often don't have — a father who is _present_, not just providing. And symbolically, he is Gest-X (the browser extension): the always-on layer, the quiet accessibility guardian who is _there_ every time you open a website, without needing you to notice him. Olo is the ambient presence.

**Where he shows up.**

- **Gest-X (browser extension)** — his home. The icon in your toolbar, the presence-figure in the extension's onboarding, the character whose voice explains what the extension does when you install it. Olo says: "I'll be here. I'll sign whatever you need." That's the vibe.
- **Parent mode / family dashboard** — the father-side of the family view. When Mme Béatrice logs into her parent account, she sees Olo on the character side because Olo is the model for what a learning-parent looks like.
- **Support / help flows** — when the app needs a calm, reassuring "here's how to fix this" tone, Olo delivers it.
- **Enterprise / MINESEC materials** — for older-adult-facing communications (ministry meetings, teacher orientations), Olo is our face because he reads as competent, calm, and non-threatening to a hearing adult audience.

**Dressing.** Indigo short-sleeve button shirt with a subtle African geometric print. Beige chinos. Brown loafers. Simple wristwatch. Neat short hair, well-trimmed beard. He dresses like a Cameroonian professional father — comfortable, respectful, understated.

**Signing role.** Olo signs like a competent adult learner. His pace is measured, his handshapes are correct, his non-manuals are slightly less pronounced than Lea's — because he is still learning, and we show that. He is the model for "you can start today as an adult and get here." He is a full 3D performer but his role in the app is smaller than the other three — he appears where his presence matters most.

## 4. Product ↔ Character mapping (canonical)

| Product                                  | Primary character | Secondary                               | Why                                                                                                      |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------- | --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Gestlingua** (translator)              | **Uria**          | Gest (male-voice option)                | The public face. Uria's articulated pace makes her the best signer for arbitrary translated text.        |
| **Gestaula** (schools)                   | **Lea**           | Uria + Gest (student examples)          | The teacher IS the LMS. Lea introduces every course.                                                     |
| **Gestsolo** (learner app)               | **Gest**          | Uria (conversation partner)             | The kid-companion who learns with the user.                                                              |

**Rule of thumb:** every product needs a face. The face is chosen by the _emotional tone_ of that product's core moment, not by content-type. The translator is bright and clear → Uria. The LMS is patient and structured → Lea. The learner app is playful and buddy-driven → Gest. The extension is quiet and always-on → Olo. The credential is authoritative and proud → Lea.

## 5. Consistency rules — do not violate

These are the rules that make four characters read as one family and one brand. Every generated image, every 3D render, every marketing shot must honor them.

1. **All four share the same skin-tone family.** Warm medium-brown, subsurface, matched across renders. When we generate Uria after Gest, we feed Gest's image to the model as a reference so the tones lock.
2. **All four have the large-expressive-eye Pixar/DreamWorks proportions.** Non-manual markers (eyebrow raises, mouth shapes) carry grammar in ASL — the faces MUST support that. A stylistic house choice AND a functional one.
3. **All four have separated, individually-modelled fingers.** They sign. Their hands must articulate. No mitten-hands, ever.
4. **All four stand in relaxed symmetrical A-pose in canonical renders.** So the mesh auto-rigs. Sign-language avatars need clean rigs more than any other kind of 3D character.
5. **Each has their signature color somewhere in the fit.** Not head-to-toe. A shirt, a cardigan, a headwrap, a hoodie. Signal, not costume.
6. **Toghu / traditional Cameroonian accents are subtle.** Cuff trim, collar embroidery, a headwrap, a shirt print. Never head-to-toe traditional dress. We are contemporary Cameroonians, not a folklore exhibit.
7. **They are a family.** Lea and Olo can appear affectionate. Gest and Uria can appear siblings — mildly competitive, deeply loyal. Never depict them as coworkers, classmates, or strangers.

## 6. Rodin / Gemini generation prompts

These are the locked prompts. Every 3D render is generated from these. Workflow:

1. Paste the prompt into **Gemini/Imagen** to get a clean 2D concept image.
2. Upload that image to **Rodin → Image-to-3D**. This gives far better fidelity than text-to-3D and keeps the four characters visually consistent as a family.
3. Generate **Gest first.** For Uria, Lea, and Olo, feed Gest's image alongside the prompt as a style reference — add "same art style, proportions, and skin-tone family as the reference image" to each subsequent prompt.
4. Ask Rodin for **A-pose or T-pose** output so the mesh auto-rigs cleanly. Sign language needs clean rigs.

## 8. Never do

- **Never** show any of the four holding a phone or a laptop as their identifier. Their identifier is their hands. Their hands sign. That is what the brand is.
- **Never** show them in stereotyped "African village" backgrounds. They live in modern Cameroon — Yaoundé, Douala, wherever the story requires. Contemporary, not folklore.
- **Never** cast them as beneficiaries of hearing help. Gesturia's characters are the experts, the guides, the teachers. Hearing users learn from them, not the other way around.
- **Never** silence them. If a character appears in a product, they sign. If we can't render their signing performance, we don't show them until we can.
- **Never** use them for anything outside Gesturia's mission. Not sponsorships, not co-brand promotions, not ads for anyone else. The characters are the brand. They stay ours.
