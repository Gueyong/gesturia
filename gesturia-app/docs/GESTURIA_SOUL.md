# GESTURIA — The Soul

> A world where sign language is a first-class citizen of the internet.
> **Gesturia. A country of gestures. Every sign belongs somewhere.**

This is the canonical brand + vision document, as written by the founder (2026-07-04).
Nothing ships that contradicts it.

## 1. Why Gesturia exists

In Cameroon there are more than 300,000 deaf people. Fewer than 1% of them have full access to
formal education. The classrooms exist. The teachers exist. The technology exists. What is missing
is the bridge — the moment where a hearing world stops being a wall and starts being a place a deaf
child can walk into.

Gesturia is not a translation app. It is the answer to that quiet, daily exclusion. We do not build
tools that "help the disabled." We build tools that make the disability go away — because the
disability was never in the person. It was in the platform, the classroom, the browser, the video
call. Gesturia rebuilds the software.

## 2. The name

**Gesturia** — from *gesture* (the language) and *-uria* (a place, a territory). A country of
gestures. A homeland for signed language, on the internet. Every surface is designed from the deaf
user outward, with the hearing world adapting to them. We inverted the default — that is the entire
product philosophy in one sentence.

Consistency rules: same warm medium-brown skin family; large expressive Pixar-style eyes (non-manual
markers carry ASL grammar); separated, individually-modelled fingers (they SIGN — no mitten hands,
ever); relaxed A-pose canonical renders; signature color as signal, not costume; subtle toghu
accents (contemporary Cameroon, never folklore); they are a FAMILY. Never: phones/laptops as their
identifier, stereotyped village backdrops, casting them as beneficiaries of hearing help, showing
them silent, or using them outside Gesturia's mission.

Roadmap cast: Sami (deaf teen), Mama Ngono (grandmother/stories), Prof (elder teacher), Kip (little
brother/humor), Nya (shy beginner).

## 5. What Gesturia will NOT do

No gimmicks — avatar performance ships at native-signer quality or not at all. Never hearing-first.
Never centralized on one sign language (ASL first for data; LSAF is where the mission begins; then
the world). Never sell out the community — deaf users' data is not for sale. Never lose the human
center — every product ships with the family, not a logo.

## 6. Trajectory

Today: Cameroon; INJS Yaoundé first school; MINESEC first partner; Healing Streams live services as
the flagship outreach broadcast. 6 months: Gestlingua public at gesturia.cm; Gestaula in 5 schools;
Gestsolo mobile; first 1,000 Gestificates; Gest-X closed beta. 18 months: Gest-X on Chrome/Firefox/
Edge; LSAF fluent; every INJS-affiliated school. 5 years: every major African sign language served.
10 years: deaf isolation on the internet is over.
